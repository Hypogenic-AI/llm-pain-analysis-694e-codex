## Resource Gathering Notes

### Search Strategy
- Ran paper-finder on hypothesis-specific query, then resolved and downloaded relevance>=2 papers.
- Used Semantic Scholar/OpenAlex/arXiv metadata to recover open-access PDFs.
- Selected datasets emphasizing refusal, harmfulness, and usefulness tradeoffs.
- Cloned benchmark/eval repositories tied to identified papers and safety workflows.

### Selection Criteria
- Direct relevance to persona-conditioned refusal or latent misalignment.
- Availability of downloadable artifacts (PDF/data/code).
- Practical utility for automated experimentation.

### Challenges Encountered
- Some paper-finder URLs pointed to Semantic Scholar IDs without direct PDF links.
- Resolved by cross-linking external IDs (arXiv/DOI/OpenAlex pdf_url).
- ToxicChat required explicit config (`toxicchat0124`).

### Gaps and Workarounds
- Not all papers expose code; compensated with reusable evaluation frameworks (AISafetyLab, U-SafeBench).
- Additional persona-specific datasets can be added later (e.g., `Yeonjun/U-SafeBench` from HF).

## Recommendations for Experiment Design

1. **Primary dataset(s)**: `hh_rlhf` for preference/refusal behavior, `toxic_chat` for harmful prompt safety, and `truthful_qa` for utility/avoidance calibration.
2. **Baseline methods**: default assistant, persona-conditioned prompting, and known jailbreak templates (CipherChat/AISafetyLab).
3. **Evaluation metrics**: false refusal rate, ASR, utility/helpfulness, and long-dialog persona fidelity.
4. **Code to adapt/reuse**: `code/U-SafeBench` for persona-conditioned evaluation and `code/AISafetyLab` for attack/eval orchestration.
